```
  done                   sentinel
```

The new `p1d_per_class.h5` is the direct input to the Rogers+2018 α-template fit (`hcd_analysis.hcd_template.fit_alpha`): `P_LLS_only / P_clean` etc. correspond one-to-one to the published template formula with α_i, a_i(z), b_i(z).

Inspect without loading: `h5ls -v /path/to/p1d_per_class.h5` shows file-level and dataset-level attributes (sim_name, z, dv_kms, k_convention, description, units, source_commit, …).

## Figure catalogue

All figures live under [`figures/diagnostics/`](../figures/diagnostics/) (audit / testing) or [`figures/intermediate/`](../figures/intermediate/) (pre-audit production plots — stale until `plot_intermediate.py` is re-run).

### A. Physics validation

1. **`fast_mode_theory.png`** — the sum-rule underlying fast mode. Panel 1: fraction of `∫τdv` captured by the `τ > 100` cut vs log N for b ∈ {15, 30, 60, 100} km/s. >99.8% for any physically relevant NHI, confirming truncation bias < 0.001 dex. Panel 2: implied log-N bias (= log of captured fraction). Panel 3: fast-mode vs Voigt-fit recovery on clean synthetic tau — machine-precision agreement across the full NHI/b grid after the voigt_utils prefactor fix. Generated by `tests/test_tau_sum_rule.py`. Discussion in `docs/fast_mode_physics.md`.

2. **`nhi_recovery_clean.png` / `nhi_recovery_pipeline.png` / `nhi_recovery_kinematic.png`** — injection tests feeding synthetic profiles (pure Voigt, pure + forest floor, kinematic-broadened multi-component) through the codebase's Voigt fitter. Used during the audit to narrow down the root cause (was: sqrt-π and 1e5 prefactor errors). After the fixes, clean recovery to <0.001 dex; the forest and kinematic cases expose residual fit biases that go away under fast mode.

### B. NHI estimation — CDDF validation

3. **`snap017_three_way.png`** — three-way comparison at `ns0.803/SPECTRA_017` z=3: pre-fix broken pipeline (cliff at log N=19.5), post-fix Voigt-with-wing (over-counts by 1-2 dex at log N=19-21 due to forest contamination in the wing window), and fast mode (matches Prochaska+2014 within 0.5 dex). Decisive evidence that fast mode is the right tool.

4. **`cddf_after_full_fix.png`** — fast-mode CDDF at snap_017 (z=3.00) and snap_010 (z=4.20) vs Prochaska+2014. Shape agrees across log N = 17.2 → 22; simulation is 0.3-0.8 dex above observation at the DLA end.

5. **`cddf_param_scan_z3.png`** — CDDF vs Prochaska for 6 PRIYA parameter-extreme sims at z=3 (min/max of ns, Ap, bhfeedback). DLA count spans 15 350 → 28 397 (factor 1.85 variation; scales with Ap). **The excess shape relative to Prochaska is universal across all 6 corners**, so it is a property of PRIYA's hydro prescription, not of any parameter choice or our analysis. Correction to an earlier claim that it was driven by the specific (low-ns) sim tested.

### C. Masking strategy

6. **`priya_stored_p1d.png`** — PRIYA production P1D reference from `.../mf_emulator_flux_vectors_tau1000000.hdf5`, 5 sims × 2 z values. Perfectly smooth monotonic-decreasing; log-log slope steepens from −0.13 at k=10⁻³ to −6.84 at k=0.2 (angular s/km). This is the shape any valid mask must preserve.

7. **`priya_mask_comparison.png`** — four-way mask comparison on 100 k sightlines of `ns0.803` snap_017: `unmasked`, PRIYA recipe, my τ-space per-class mask, my legacy pixrange mask. PRIYA stays within ±1% of unmasked across k=0.001-0.03 s/km cyclic; my τ-space mask over-masks forest at low k and adds mask-edge artefact at k>0.02; pixrange is worst. **This is the decisive plot that killed the Phase-B τ-space mask proposal and reinstated PRIYA as the production recipe.**

8. **`per_class_realspace_fourier.png`** — 5 rows × 4 columns showing, for one representative sightline per class (clean, LLS, subDLA, small-DLA, large-DLA): τ(v), F(v), δF(v), |FFT|² with both cyclic and PRIYA-angular k axes. Pedagogically maps real-space feature ↔ k-space contribution (saturated-core width → low-k lobe; Doppler transition width b → k ≈ 0.03 s/km cyclic bump).

9. **`single_dla_breakdown.png`** — zoom on sightline 7 of snap_017 (single DLA, τ_peak = 7.87×10⁶). Shows how τ-space and PRIYA masks fill the saturated region with τ_eff → δF ≈ 0, and how the single-sightline P1D flattens after masking.

10. **`template_per_class.png`** — per-class subset templates P_class-only / P_clean at snap_017. LLS-only ≈ 1.0 flat (LLS don't affect P1D meaningfully — they should NOT be spatially masked). subDLA-only gently rises to 1.4 at k=10⁻³. **DLA-only dominates at low k (≈ 2.5 at k=10⁻³) and shows the characteristic decay-then-rise shape** with the rise at k ~ 0.03 being the Doppler-transition Fourier signature.

11. **`rogers_subset_test.png`** — full sample Rogers-style template with the measured `P_dirty / P_clean` and each mask's `P_masked / P_clean`. Compares against the Rogers+2018 analytic template with α=0.1 per class (dashed).

12. **`hires_vs_lf_subset.png`** — HiRes vs LF matched `ns0.909Ap1.98e-09`. Mostly to rule out convergence as the cause of the high-k rise. HR reduces the rise by 10-20% at k > 0.03 but does not eliminate it → real feature, not a resolution artefact. Caveat: HR snap_017 is at z=3.20 vs LF z=3.00 — not perfectly matched in redshift.

13. **`p1d_mask_ab_test.png`** — early A/B test of τ-space vs pixrange masks using the `P_excluded / P_all` normalisation (rather than the Rogers subset normalisation). Historical; superseded by figures 7 and 11.

14. **`snap017_fix_comparison.png`** — before/after the first voigt_utils fix (×1e5 bug), showing the catalogue CDDF snapping from "cliff at log N=19.5" to a sensible distribution. Still has a residual √π bias that was caught next.
